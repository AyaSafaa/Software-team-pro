Paris (French: Paris) is the capital and largest city of France by population. It is located on the banks of the Seine River in the northern part of the country in the heart of the Ile-de-France region. Its population reached 2,243,833 in 2010 within its administrative scope only, while the population of the city and its suburbs exceeded 12 million people. Paris had been a highly important region for more than two thousand years, and by the turn of the twelfth century, Paris had become a European center of finance, diplomacy, trade, fashion, science and the arts, and the largest city in the Western world until the early eighteenth century. Paris has been the scene of many important political events throughout history, such as the French Revolution. Nowadays, it is one of the largest economic and cultural centers with an important influence in politics, science, entertainment, media, fashion and the arts, making it one of the world's major cities. In 2011, the city's GDP was 607 billion euros ($ 845 billion) and is one of the largest domestic products of cities in the world. Paris is one of the leading tourist destinations in the world as it is the headquarters of most French companies. Paris has a variety of museums, theaters and monuments built over the centuries, such as the Eiffel Tower, the Arc de Triomphe, the Louvre and the Palace of Versailles. Paris is one of the largest centers of art in the world, with a large number of museums that contain paintings of the most prominent international artists. The city's cuisine has a global reputation, as it attracts the most famous chefs in the world. Paris and its environs include the most prestigious French schools and universities, as well as the headquarters of the largest French newspapers, such as: Le Monde, Le Figaro, and Liberation. Paris is the headquarters of the Paris Saint-Germain football club, and there is also the stadium in France, which was built to host the 1998 FIFA World Cup.
